# Update-Service Hymn Slot Repair

Timestamp: 2026-04-24 09:26:04

## Summary

This update fixes incorrect hymn rendering in `update-service` where many services were showing hymns as `Other Hymn` even though the database still preserved their real service positions.

## Problem

- Some services in `hymn_usage_db` had rows whose `slot_id` pointed to `Other Hymn`, even though their `sort_order` still matched normal fixed positions in the service.
- The `update-service` hymn-state builder was reconstructing the editor mostly from slot-name occurrence counts.
- That caused legitimate fixed-position hymns such as Opening, Chief, Distribution, and Closing hymns to fall into `Other Hymn` or extra-row handling for affected services.

## Fix

- Updated `update-service.php` so hymn rows still use the stored slot when it is specific and trustworthy.
- Added a fallback repair path for legacy/corrupted `Other Hymn` rows:
  - if the row can be safely mapped by its saved `sort_order` into the current service-setting definition,
  - it is restored to that fixed hymn position instead of remaining `Other Hymn`.
- Kept true extra hymns and real `Other Hymn` behavior intact where the row cannot be safely repaired into a standard slot.

## Result

- Affected services now retain their proper hymn use and position in `update-service`.
- Services that already rendered correctly continue to use their existing slot mapping.
- The fix uses the saved `slot_id` and `sort_order` together, as intended.

## Verification

- `php -l update-service.php` passes.
- Live rendered hymn state was spot-checked against local DB-backed services including `110` and `118`.
- Those services now populate standard hymn slots in the editor instead of collapsing into `Other Hymn` rows.
